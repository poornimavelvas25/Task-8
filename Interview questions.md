# Interview Questions

## 1.What is a VPN?

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) is a service that creates a secure connection between your device and the internet.
It hides your real IP address by replacing it with the IP address of the VPN server.
This makes it appear as if you are browsing from a different location.
A VPN encrypts your internet traffic so others cannot easily see what you are doing online.
It is commonly used for privacy, security, and accessing restricted content.

## 2️.How does a VPN protect privacy?

A VPN protects privacy by masking your real IP address.
When your IP is hidden, websites cannot directly track your actual location.
It encrypts your internet data so your ISP or hackers cannot read it.
This is especially useful when using public Wi-Fi networks.
However, your privacy also depends on whether the VPN provider keeps logs or not.

## 3️.Difference between VPN and Proxy?

A VPN encrypts all internet traffic from your device (or browser if using extension).
A proxy usually hides your IP address but does not encrypt your traffic.
VPNs provide stronger security compared to proxies.
Proxies are mainly used to bypass website restrictions.
VPNs are better for privacy and secure communication.

## 4️.What is encryption in VPN?

Encryption is the process of converting readable data into unreadable code.
When you use a VPN, your data is encrypted before it leaves your device.
This means hackers or ISPs cannot understand your traffic even if they intercept it.
Most VPNs use strong encryption like AES-256.
Encryption ensures confidentiality and protects sensitive information.

## 5️.Can VPN guarantee complete anonymity?

No, a VPN cannot guarantee 100% anonymity.
It hides your IP address but does not make you invisible online.
Websites can still track you using cookies or account logins.
If the VPN provider keeps logs, your activity may still be recorded.
For complete anonymity, additional tools like Tor may be needed.

## 6️.What protocols do VPNs use?

VPNs use secure protocols to create encrypted tunnels.
Common protocols include OpenVPN, WireGuard, and IKEv2.
OpenVPN is widely used and very secure.
WireGuard is newer and offers faster speeds with strong security.
The protocol affects both security and performance.

## 7.What are some VPN limitations?

Free VPNs often have limited data and slower speeds.
Some VPNs may log user activity.
VPNs cannot protect against viruses or phishing attacks.
They may not work with all websites or streaming services.
Using a VPN may also reduce internet speed.

## 8️.How does a VPN affect network speed?

A VPN can slow down your internet connection.
This happens because data is encrypted and routed through another server.
If the server is far away, latency increases.
Free VPN servers are often crowded, which reduces speed.
However, premium VPNs with nearby servers may have minimal speed loss.
